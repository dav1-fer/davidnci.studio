Genesis Collection

The poster visually represents the concept of creation and the connection between humans and God, as depicted in the Book of Genesis from the Bible. In Genesis, God creates the universe, Earth, and all living beings, culminating in the creation of humans, made in His own image. This profound connection between the divine and humanity is symbolized in the poster through the imagery of two hands reaching towards each other, reminiscent of Michelangelo's famous "Creation of Adam" fresco.
One hand, emerging from a cosmic background, symbolizes the divine act of creation, while the other hand, extended from a cloud, represents God's continual presence and connection with humanity. The olive branch in the human hand is a symbol of peace, life, and renewal, reflecting the promise of a new beginning after the flood in the story of Noah's Ark.
Overall, the poster encapsulates the themes of creation, divine-human connection, and the ongoing relationship between God and humanity, central to the narrative of Genesis.
